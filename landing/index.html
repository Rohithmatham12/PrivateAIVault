How it works

Three steps, all provable, none of them exposing your data

Every step below is a real execution: an actual LLM call, an actual SHA-256 hash, an actual compiled zero-knowledge circuit. Nothing here is simulated for the demo.

1

AI redaction

An LLM scans the raw record and strips names, SSNs, emails, phone numbers, and medical/financial identifiers, producing a version that's safe to display.

2

Commit a hash, not the data

A SHA-256 commitment of the original record is computed locally. Only that 32-byte hash is ever sent to the smart contract. The record itself never is.

3

Verify without revealing

Anyone holding a candidate original can recompute its hash and check it against the on-chain commitment, proving a match without exposing the candidate to anyone.

Who this is for

Anywhere confidentiality and an audit trail are both non-negotiable

The same pattern (prove a record exists and is unaltered, without storing or exposing it) applies wherever sensitive text meets a compliance requirement.

Healthcare

Prove a patient record existed in a given state for a compliance or insurance dispute, without PHI ever touching a public ledger.

Legal & notarization

Timestamp-prove a contract or filing hasn't been altered, without publishing its contents to anyone.

Fintech KYC

Prove an identity document matches what was verified at onboarding, without storing the ID data anywhere it can later be breached.

HR & background checks

Prove a screening ran against a specific, unaltered document without retaining the document itself.

Journalism & whistleblowing

Prove a leaked document is authentic and unmodified without exposing it, or its source, until the holder chooses to.

Any regulated free text

AI redaction makes this practical for real-world documents such as medical notes and intake forms, not just pre-structured data.

Under the hood

Nothing here is a mock

commitRecord and verifyMatchesCommitment are compiled to real zero-knowledge circuits by the Compact compiler. The live demo bundles the compiled contract and Midnight's compact-runtime to WebAssembly and runs it directly in your browser tab.
